# Vision

ZIP-first is not merely “fill city from ZIP.” It is a small rebellion against address forms that ignore the information structure of addresses.

Good address UX should:

- ask high-information fields early;
- use standards-compatible browser autofill tokens;
- avoid making the user retype inferable data;
- make inferred fields visible and editable;
- distinguish postal routing from legal jurisdiction;
- degrade cleanly across countries;
- send as little personal data as possible.

The extension is the wedge. The real win is getting checkout libraries, forms, government services, and design systems to reorder fields natively.

## Slogan candidates

- ZIP first. Street last. Humans spared.
- Fill the boring parts.
- Postal defaults, not postal traps.
- Address forms, less cursed.
- Ask the field that knows the most.
